Annual Camp Meeting
Zambia SDA Union · All Conferences Welcome

"They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to fellowship, to the breaking of bread and to prayer." — Acts 2:42

What Is Camp Meeting?

Camp Meeting is the most important spiritual gathering of the Adventist year. Thousands of believers from across Zambia gather for a week of Bible preaching, worship, prayer, fellowship, and spiritual renewal. Children, youth, and adults all have dedicated programmes. It is where God moves powerfully and many are baptised.

What to Bring

Bible and notebook · Bedding (tent or accommodation) · Modest Adventist dress · Food contribution for communal meals · Offering · A heart ready to hear from God.

📋 Registration Process
Step 1: Contact your church elder or church clerk for the registration form.
Step 2: Complete the form with your full name, church, district, and conference.
Step 3: Pay the registration fee at your church treasurer (varies by conference).
Step 4: Receive your camp meeting badge and accommodation assignment.
Step 5: Arrive one day before camp begins to set up and settle in.
🏕️ Accommodation Options
Tent space — bring your own tent, allocated per family/group.
Church dormitory — shared sleeping quarters, book early.
VIP rooms — limited private rooms for elderly/disabled members.
Day visitor — attend sessions only, no overnight stay required.
📞 Register Through Your Church

Registration opens approximately 3 months before camp meeting. Speak to your Church Clerk or Head Elder to begin the process. Check the Activities page for your church's camp meeting dates.

Pathfinder Club
Ages 10–15 · Building disciples, one honour at a time
About Pathfinders

The Pathfinder Club is the official youth programme of the Seventh-day Adventist Church for children aged 10–15. It combines Bible study, practical skills (honours), community service, camping, and marching into a powerful character-development journey. Every Adventist young person should experience Pathfinders.

📋 How to Register
Step 1: Talk to your parents/guardians — they must agree and support your membership.
Step 2: Visit your church on a Friday afternoon (club meeting time) and speak to the Pathfinder Director.
Step 3: Complete the membership form with your parent or guardian.
Step 4: Pay the annual membership fee (ask your director for the current amount).
Step 5: Receive your investiture class assignment and begin earning honours!
Step 6: Attend the District Camporee — one of the best experiences of your life.
🎖️ Adventurer Club (Ages 6–9)

Younger children (6–9) can join the Adventurer Club — the Pathfinder programme for little ones. Same structure, age-appropriate activities. Speak to the Children's Ministry Coordinator to register.

Personal soul-winning: Ask your head elder to train you in personal Bible study methods. Training takes 2–3 sessions.

Sabbath School
Every Sabbath · 9:30 AM · All Ages

"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path." — Psalm 119:105

What Is Sabbath School?

Sabbath School is the Bible study programme of the Seventh-day Adventist Church, held every Sabbath morning before the main worship service. It is one of the oldest and most important institutions of the church. It is where members grow in Scripture knowledge, build community, and reach out to the world.

Sabbath School Divisions

📋 How to Enrol
Step 1: Arrive at church by 9:30 AM any Sabbath morning.
Step 2: Find the Sabbath School Secretary and give them your name, age, and division.
Step 3: You will be assigned to the correct division and given a quarterly lesson booklet.
Step 4: Attend every week. Regular attendance earns you a Attendance Award at year end.
Step 5: Participate — discussion, prayer, and mission offerings are all part of Sabbath School.
📘 The Quarterly Lesson

Every quarter, the worldwide Adventist Church studies the same Bible topic together. You can get your copy from your Sabbath School Secretary or purchase one from the ABC (Adventist Book Centre). Read the lesson daily — each day has a short passage to study.

🌍 Mission Offerings

Every Sabbath School collects mission offerings that fund Adventist work worldwide — building schools, hospitals, and churches in areas without resources. Your offering, however small, reaches the ends of the earth.
